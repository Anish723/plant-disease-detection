Late Blight

Cause:
Late blight is caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans which spreads quickly in cool and humid environments.

Symptoms:
Dark brown or black lesions appear on leaves and stems. White fungal growth may appear on the underside of leaves.

Treatment:
Use fungicides such as chlorothalonil or copper fungicide. Remove infected leaves immediately.

Prevention:
Avoid overhead irrigation and maintain good airflow between plants.

Early Blight

Cause:
Early blight is caused by the fungus Alternaria solani.

Symptoms:
Brown circular spots with concentric rings appear on leaves.

Treatment:
Apply fungicides and remove infected leaves.

Prevention:
Practice crop rotation and maintain healthy soil nutrients.

Leaf Mold

Cause:
Leaf mold is caused by the fungus Passalora fulva.

Symptoms:
Yellow spots appear on the upper surface of leaves while mold develops underneath.

Treatment:
Apply fungicide and reduce humidity.

Prevention:
Improve ventilation and avoid excessive watering.

Healthy Plant Care

Tips:
Maintain balanced fertilizer levels, proper sunlight exposure, and consistent watering schedules.

Prevention:
Healthy plants are more resistant to diseases when soil nutrition and watering are balanced.
